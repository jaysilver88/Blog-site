Aphorisms

Progress in any aspect is a movement through changes of terminology.

Wisdom asks nothing more.

Authors are actors, books are theatres.

Poetry and materia poetica are interchangeable terms.

A new meaning is the equivalent of a new word.

The romantic cannot be seen through: it is for the moment willingly not seen through.

The poem reveals itself only to the ignorant man.

There are two opposites: the poetry of rhetoric and the poetry of experience.

We live in the mind.

It is not every day that the world arranges itself in a poem.

In the presence of extraordinary actuality, consciousness takes the place of imagination.

To live in the world but outside of existing conceptions of it.

Poetry is a poetic conception, however expressed. A poem is poetry expressed in words. But in a poem there is a poetry of words. Obviously, a poem may consist of several poetries.

The aesthetic order includes all other orders but is not limited to them.

The eye sees less than the tongue says. The tongue says less than the mind thinks.

The body is the great poem.

Poetry must resist the intelligence almost successfully.

Description is an element, like air or water.

It is manner that becomes stale.

The fundamental difficulty in any art is the problem of the normal.
